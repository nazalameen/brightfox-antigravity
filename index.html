Robotics & Coding Curriculum

Our courses are meticulously structured into age-appropriate stages, guiding students from fundamental mechanical creation to IoT and Artificial Intelligence.

Ages 5 - 8
Level: Beginner 3 Months

Mechano Explorer

Perfect entry for young builders. Kids learn mechanical design using gears, levers, pulleys, and simple structural beams to build motorized models. Builds fine motor skills and spatial reasoning.

What they build:
  • Motorized Conveyor Belts
  • Windmills & Solar-tracked Fans
  • Wired Remote control Cars
Interactive Class
Ages 8 - 10
Level: Novice 4 Months

Elex Inventor

Introducing children to the invisible magic of electricity. They build safe electronic circuits on solderless breadboards using LEDs, buzzers, capacitors, potentiometers, and ambient light sensors.

What they build:
  • Automatic Night Lamps
  • Fire Alarm & Rain Detector Systems
  • Electronic Piano Keyboards

Why Choose Us

More Than Just Coding

Building robots helps kids develop multiple high-value cognitive and soft skills that prepare them for success in any career path.

Logical Problem Solving

When a robot does not run correctly, kids learn to trace bugs logically, test assumptions, and learn from mistakes without frustration.

Hands-on Creativity

Turning abstract thoughts into mechanical real-world structures teaches kids how to combine design thinking with mathematical precision.

Collaboration & Teamwork

Students work in pairs or groups to resolve mechanical tasks, assigning roles, discussing code logic, and achieving milestones together.

Confidence Booster

Completing physical, functional robot models provides kids a huge sense of self-belief and boosts confidence in their academic pursuits.

Learning Journey

How Kids Learn at Brightfox

Our project-based learning model is centered around the natural discovery process, fostering structural, analytical, and communications skills.

1

1. Discover & Build

Every class starts with a structural puzzle. Students assemble real gears, sensors, and structural items, exploring mechanical physics firsthand.

2

2. Program & Code

Using Scratch block-coding or Arduino C++, kids create the robotic logic. They learn loops, conditional statements, variables, and algorithms.

3

3. Test & Calibrate

We test our robots in real-world setups like maze courses and tracking maps. Students adjust threshold parameters to perfect their robot's behavior.

4

4. Present & Compete

Students present their robots to parents and peers. Friendly classroom challenges and annual competitions motivate kids to push design boundaries.
